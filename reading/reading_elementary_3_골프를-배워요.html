On a bright afternoon, a young boy named Tom decided to learn about golf. He always had a curious heart. He asked his dad, "Can you teach me how to play golf?". His dad, a great golf player, was happy and said, "Of course, Tom! Let's begin." The next day, they went to a large green field, the golf course. It was full of people of all ages, but Tom was not scared. He was excited and ready to learn. His dad gave him a golf club. It was heavier than Tom thought, but he held it with both hands. His dad said, "Golf is not just about hitting the ball hard. It's about how you control the ball with your club. Always keep your eye on the ball and feel the air around you." Tom listened carefully and nodded. He put the small white ball on the ground. He took a deep breath, raised his club, and swung it. The ball flew across the field. It was not a big hit, but Tom was happy. Again and again, Tom swung the club. At first, he missed the ball, but he didn't get angry. He learned that golf is not easy. It's a game of patience and control. He felt a sense of joy and satisfaction each time he hit the ball. In the end, Tom enjoyed his golf lesson. His body was tired but his heart was full of happiness. He thanked his dad and said, "I had so much fun today! I can't wait to play golf again." From that day, Tom practiced golf every afternoon. He learned that golf is not just a game, but also an art. An art of patience, control, and joy. He knew he was not the best, but he was getting better every day. And that's what made golf so much fun for Tom.
