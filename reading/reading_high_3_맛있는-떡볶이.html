Once upon a time, in a big city, there was a boy who loved delicious food. He always had a special love for his mom's cooking. But out of all dishes, his favorite was spicy rice cake, also known as "tteokbokki". One afternoon, the boy was at home. His tummy began to feel hungry. He thought about his mom's tteokbokki. His mouth began to water at the thought. He could almost taste the spicy and sweet sauce, and the chewy rice cakes. He decided he would ask his mom to make it for dinner. He ran into the kitchen, where his mom was. With a big smile on his face, he said, "Mom, can we have tteokbokki for dinner?" His mom looked at him and smiled back. "Of course, my son!" she answered. Together, they began to make the tteokbokki. His mom showed him how to prepare the rice cakes. She took out a bottle of red pepper paste from the fridge. The boy watched as she mixed it with water and sugar to make the sauce. He could already smell the spicy aroma fill the air around them. While the sauce was cooking, his mom added the rice cakes. The boy could hear the rice cakes sizzling in the hot sauce. His heart was filled with joy and excitement. He knew it would be a wonderful dinner. Finally, the moment he was waiting for arrived. His mom served the tteokbokki in a large bowl. The boy could see the steam rising from the hot and spicy dish. He could hardly wait to taste it. He took a big bite. It was just as he had imagined. The rice cakes were chewy, and the sauce was a perfect mix of spicy and sweet. He felt warm and happy. He looked at his mom and said, "Mom, your tteokbokki is the best in the world!" From that day on, the boy often helped his mom cook tteokbokki. He enjoyed every moment of it. He learned that cooking was not just about eating, but also about spending time with family and making happy memories.
